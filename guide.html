How to Use Stream Filter

Adjust brightness, contrast, and color while watching videos — no installation required.

Quick Start

1 Copy a video URL

Copy the URL from YouTube, Twitch, Vimeo, or Dailymotion. Any standard video or clip URL will work.

2 Paste and Play

Paste the URL into the input field on Stream Filter and click "Play" (or press Enter).

3 Adjust filters

Use the sliders to adjust brightness, contrast, saturation, and more in real time. Try a preset for instant results.

Available Filters

FilterRangeWhat it does
Brightness30% – 500%Makes the video brighter or darker. Great for dark scenes or night viewing.
Contrast30% – 500%Increases or decreases the difference between light and dark areas.
Saturation0% – 500%Controls color intensity. 0% = grayscale, higher = more vivid colors.
Hue Rotate0° – 360°Shifts all colors around the color wheel. Use for color temperature adjustments.
Sepia0% – 100%Adds a warm, brownish tone. Comfortable for long viewing sessions.
Blur0 – 10pxSoftens the image. Useful for background/ambient playback.

Tips

  • Night Mode + lower brightness is the easiest way to reduce eye strain at night.
  • Vivid preset works great for nature documentaries and cooking videos.
  • Use Hue Rotate to shift the overall color temperature — try 15–30° for a warmer tone.
  • Click "Reset" to instantly return all filters to their default values.
  • You can combine presets with manual adjustments — pick a preset first, then fine-tune.

FAQ

Is Stream Filter free?

Yes, completely free. No account, no installation, no ads (yet).

Does it work on mobile?

Yes! Stream Filter is fully responsive and works on phones and tablets.

Is my data collected?

No. All filters are applied locally in your browser using CSS. Nothing is sent to any server. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Why does a video show an error?

Some videos have embedding disabled by their owners. In that case, the video cannot be loaded in Stream Filter. Try a different video.

Can I use this for live streams?

Yes! Twitch live streams and YouTube live streams are supported.
