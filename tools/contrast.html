What Is Video Contrast?

Contrast is the difference between the brightest and darkest parts of an image. Low contrast makes a video look flat and washed out, while high contrast adds depth, making colors richer and details sharper.

When to Adjust Contrast

Washed-Out Videos
Increase contrast to restore depth and definition.
Foggy / Hazy Footage
Cut through haze with higher contrast settings.
Cinematic Look
Boost contrast for a dramatic, movie-like feel.
Comfortable Viewing
Lower contrast for a softer image during long sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does contrast adjustment do to a video?

Contrast adjustment increases or decreases the difference between the lightest and darkest parts of the video. Higher contrast makes colors pop and details stand out, while lower contrast creates a softer, more muted look.

How do I fix a washed-out video?

Increase the contrast slider on Stream Filter. This restores depth and definition to flat, washed-out footage. Combining higher contrast with slightly increased saturation often gives the best results.

Can I combine contrast with other filters?

Yes! Stream Filter lets you adjust contrast alongside brightness, saturation, hue, sepia, and blur — all at the same time. Use presets like "Vivid" for a quick combo.
