Sometimes you need to see details that are too small in the original video — reading text on a whiteboard, checking a product close-up, or analyzing gameplay footage. Browser zoom scales the entire page, but Stream Filter zooms only the video, keeping controls at normal size.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I zoom in on a YouTube video?

Open streamfilter.tv, paste any YouTube URL, and use the Zoom slider to magnify from 1x up to 10x. Drag with your mouse to pan and explore any part of the frame. Click to play/pause while zoomed.

Can I zoom in on a specific part of a video?

Yes! After zooming in, drag the video to move your viewpoint to any part of the frame. You can explore corners, read small text, or focus on details anywhere in the video.

Does zooming reduce video quality?

Zooming magnifies the existing pixels, so at very high zoom levels the image may appear pixelated. For best results, make sure the video is playing at the highest available quality (1080p or 4K) before zooming in.

Can I zoom in on videos on my phone?

Yes! Stream Filter works on mobile browsers. Use the zoom slider to magnify the video, then drag with your finger to pan around the frame. This is different from pinch-to-zoom, which zooms the entire page.

How is this different from browser zoom?

Browser zoom (Ctrl/Cmd + scroll) zooms the entire page including UI, menus, and controls. Stream Filter's zoom only magnifies the video content, keeping all controls and sliders at normal size.

Can I zoom and adjust brightness at the same time?

Yes! All Stream Filter features work together. You can zoom in while adjusting brightness, contrast, saturation, and other filters simultaneously.
